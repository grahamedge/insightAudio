About Me

I am a mathematical thinker who loves to tear apart complex concepts, messy data, and ugly equations to get at the insights hidden inside. I've trained as an experimental physicist, and I received my Ph.D in Quantum Physics in 2016 from the University of Toronto. After a brief stint of postdoctoral work, I decided that I no longer wanted to build and debug lasers so I moved to New York City to build and debug machine learning models as a Data Scientist. I'm currently a fellow at Insight Data Science.

I have a lot of experience with image analysis as a result of my Ph.D research. This includes some mundane tasks like two-dimensional least-squares fitting of various functions, as well as some more interesting techniques like an application of maximum likelihood estimation for the analysis of incredibly dim and blurry images of individual atoms. During the course of my PhD research I also trained some convolutional neural networks to perform some super-resolution image processing.

When I'm not at my laptop I can usually be found walking my scrappy little dog Basil. I also love listening to music, cooking, and doing cryptic crosswords.
